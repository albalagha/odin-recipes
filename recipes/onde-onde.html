Onde-Onde Recipe

Description

Onde-onde is a sweet pastry of Chinese origin that is popular in much of south and southeast Asia. It is made of glutinous rice flour covered in sesame seeds and usually filled with sweet bean pastes. It is commonly eaten as a snack.

Ingredients

  • 60g Knorr Potato Flakes
  • 125g refined sugar
  • 125g white glutinous rice flour
  • 65g margarine
  • 125g peeled green beans
  • 50g granulated sugar
  • 50g coconut milk
  • 2g salt
  • 2g vanilla
  • 200g white sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Heat water on the stove
  2. Add Knorr Potato Flakes. Stir until it becomes mashed potato, set aside
  3. Mix margarine, sticky rice flour, and refined sugar. Stir evenly.
  4. Add mashed potato, knead until smooth
  5. Prepare blender, add salt, vanilla, coconut milk, sugar, and peeled green beans. Blend until smooth.
  6. Heat pan.
  7. Pour the blended mixture, cook over low heat until it becomes a paste.
  8. Take the onde-onde skin mixture, thin it, and place the filling onto it.
  9. Round into ball. Repeat until the batter runs out.
  10. Dip each ball into water, then coat with sesame seeds.
  11. Fry until golden yellow.
