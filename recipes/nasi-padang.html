Nasi Padang Recipe

Description

Nasi Padang (Padang Rice in English) is a steamed rice dish from the city of Padang in West Sumatra. It is an Indonesian staple that is served and enjoyed in innumerable warungs (small family restaurants) across the country. What an individual serving looks like varies from warung to warung, and even from customer to customer, as each chef prepares a type of buffet from which customers can choose side dishes to accompany their rice.

Nasi Padang is commonly served with beef rendang (slow cooked beef in coconut milk and spices), curried fish, stewed greens, chili eggplant, beef liver, tripe, intestines, or lungs, fried chicken, and sambal. Today's recipe is going to be a simple iteration with chicken. Serves 16.

Ingredients

  • 16 chicken drumsticks
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • juice of 1 lime
  • 3 tablespoon oil
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 100 gram shallot
  • 4 cloves of garlc
  • 5-10 red cayenne chilis
  • 2 inches of ginger

Steps

  1. Grind your shallots, garlic, cayenne chilis, and ginger into a spice paste.
  2. Rub chicken with salt and lime juice in a mixing bowl. Set aside for 30 minutes, then rinse and dry.
  3. Heat oil and saute spice paste until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add bay leaves, kaffir lime leaves, coconut milk, and the chicken. Bring to a boil.
  5. Lower heat, simmer until chicken is fully cooked and the sauce is slightly reduced.
  6. Turn off heat, strain the chicken, and set aside the sauce for basting.
  7. Preheat oven broiler to high for 3 minutes.
  8. Line a baking tray with aluminum foil.
  9. Transfer chicken to prepared tray and broil until surface has charred, about 3-5 minutes on each side. Baste with sauce as needed.
  10. Serve with steamed white rice.
